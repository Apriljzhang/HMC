Refund & Cancellation Policy

Last updated: 27 May 2026

This policy explains cancellation and refund terms for services offered under the Prince Education brand and clarifies how conference registrations are handled separately.

1. Language training & corporate programmes

Unless your signed enrolment agreement states otherwise:

  • Cancellation before course start: Written notice at least 14 calendar days before the first scheduled class may qualify for a refund minus an administrative fee of 10% of tuition paid, or transfer of credit to a later intake within 6 months.
  • Cancellation within 14 days of start: No cash refund; partial credit may be offered at our discretion.
  • After course start: Fees for completed sessions are non-refundable. Unused prepaid hours may be handled per contract terms.
  • Non-refundable items: Registration deposits, examination fees paid to third parties, and bespoke materials are generally non-refundable once ordered.
  • Force majeure: If we cancel a class due to events beyond our control, you may choose a reschedule or pro-rata refund for affected sessions.

Refund requests may be submitted in writing via the contact details on the About & Contact page, including your name, contract reference, and reason. Approved refunds are processed to the original payment method within 14-30 business days.

2. AALA 2026 Conference registration

Registration information for the AALA 2026 Conference is collected on aalaconference.com. Credit card payment is processed on Prince Education via our bank payment gateway at checkout.

Refunds, cancellations, and substitutions for conference fees follow the policies published by the AALA organising committee and Prince Education's payment terms. For payment issues, contact Prince Education; for registration content issues, contact the AALA organisers.

3. Chargebacks and disputes

If you believe a charge is incorrect, contact us before initiating a bank chargeback so we can resolve the issue promptly. For conference charges, contact the AALA organisers first.
